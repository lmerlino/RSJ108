Luke Merlino's Tips on Taking and Editing Great Photos

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to take and edit great photos ...

Assembly time: 60 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Are you wondering how to take great photos that look like they were taken by a professional? Photography can be a really fun hobby to have. Why don't you try taking some photos yourself? Follow all the steps and you will have some great photos!

  • Things You'll Need

  • DSLR Camera (+lens)
  • SD Card
  • USB Cable
  • Computer
  • Lightroom

  1. Adjust your camera settings.

    To set-up your camera to take the best pictures, make sure it is shooting RAW files. The file size will be larger, but will take much better photos. Make sure the camera is in "Manual" mode. Lastly, make sure the 9x9 grid is shown on the screen.

  2. Understand and practice the exposure triangle.

    It can be daunting at first, but understanding the exposure triangle is key to taking great photos. Play around with the settings and take a few test photos until you are comfortable. Don't forget to use the light meter in your camera to help you.

    Use this diagram to practice.

  3. Choose a subject and frame it.

    Once you are comfortable with the exposure triangle, choose the subject you want to photograph. Use the rule of thirds or leading lines to make the composition more visually pleasing.

  4. Take photos.

    Congratulations, you are ready to photograph! Change the ISO, Shutter Speed, and Aperture until it is exposed properly. Don't forget to focus!

  5. Upload photos to computer.

    After you've taken some photos, upload them to your computer. You can plug it in with the USB cable, or just put your SD card in.

  6. Edit using Adobe Lightroom.

    As a rule of thumb, I like to lower the highlights and raise the shadows while editing. You can play around with other settings until you're happy. This is the most subjective part of the process.

  7. Share!

    Congratulations! You just took some great photos. If you want, you can post them online for others to see. Or maybe, you just want to keep them for yourself, and that's great too.
